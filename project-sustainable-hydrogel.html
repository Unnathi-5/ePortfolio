Sustainable Hydrogel Electrodes

Pre-doctoral Research Associate, Lehigh University

August 2025 - Present

The Challenge

Standard synthetic electrodes used in biomedical applications present significant challenges: high manufacturing costs and substantial environmental waste. Traditional disposable electrodes contribute to medical device waste, while their synthetic materials often lack biocompatibility and sustainability. The industry needed a solution that could maintain high signal fidelity while reducing both economic and environmental impact.

The Solution

I formulated novel gel-based bioelectrodes derived from agricultural surplus materials, creating a sustainable alternative to disposable synthetics. The hydrogel formulation was optimized for high-fidelity physiological data acquisition, validated against industry-standard electrodes. The flexible electrodes were fabricated using a proprietary process that transforms agricultural waste into functional biomedical interfaces, maintaining signal quality while significantly reducing environmental footprint.

Tech Stack

Materials Science Physiological Data Acquisition Validation Studies Electrode Fabrication Biocompatibility Testing

Methodology

The development process involved systematic material selection from agricultural surplus, followed by hydrogel formulation optimization. Electrochemical characterization was performed to validate signal quality, comparing against commercial Ag/AgCl electrodes. Biocompatibility studies ensured safety for clinical use, while environmental impact assessments quantified waste reduction. The final formulation underwent rigorous validation studies following ISO 13485 guidelines.
