Wearable Biosensing Platform

Pre-doctoral Research Associate, Lehigh University

August 2025 - Present

The Challenge

Continuous, untethered physiological monitoring requires a platform that can operate without noise interference while maintaining low power consumption. Existing wearable systems often suffer from signal degradation due to motion artifacts, electromagnetic interference, and power constraints. The challenge was to design a flexible, wearable system capable of high-quality biopotential acquisition with minimal noise and extended battery life for long-term monitoring applications.

The Solution

I designed a custom flexible PCB-based wearable platform integrating the MAX30003 biopotential analog front-end (AFE) with optimized LiPo power circuitry. The system architecture centered on the Nordic nRF52840 microcontroller, which I programmed for low-noise Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) data transmission and intelligent power management. The flexible PCB design enabled conformal attachment to the body, reducing motion artifacts while the custom firmware implemented adaptive sampling and power gating to extend battery life.

Tech Stack

Altium Nordic nRF52840 C++ BLE Flexible PCBs MAX30003 AFE Embedded Systems Power Management

Methodology

The development process began with PCB layout design in Altium, optimizing trace routing for signal integrity and EMI reduction. The flexible substrate selection balanced mechanical durability with electrical performance. Firmware development focused on implementing efficient BLE protocols, adaptive sampling algorithms, and power management strategies. System validation included noise floor measurements, battery life testing, and real-world wearability studies. The platform was tested across various physiological monitoring scenarios to ensure robust performance.
